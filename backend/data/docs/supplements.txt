Supplement guidance for training and recovery

Protein
- Whey: fast absorption, ideal post-workout. 20–30 g per serving.
- Casein: slow release, good before bed. 20–40 g.
- Plant (pea, rice, hemp): for vegetarians/vegans. Combine sources for complete amino acids.

Creatine monohydrate
- 5 g per day. Supports strength and power. Can load with 20 g/day for 5–7 days then 5 g maintenance, or just 5 g daily.
- Safe and well-researched for muscle and performance.

Omega-3 (fish oil)
- 2–3 g EPA+DHA combined per day. Supports recovery, inflammation, heart health.
- Take with meals for absorption.

Vitamin D
- Many people are deficient. 2000–4000 IU daily depending on levels and sun exposure. Supports immunity and bone health.

Magnesium
- 300–400 mg elemental magnesium (e.g. glycinate) can support sleep and muscle function. Often taken in the evening.

Multivitamin
- Optional if diet is varied. Choose one that covers basics without megadoses.

Pre-workout (optional)
- Caffeine 3–6 mg per kg bodyweight 30–60 min before training. Beta-alanine, citrulline may help performance; follow label dosing.

For muscle gain
- Prioritize: protein supplement if needed to hit targets, creatine, omega-3, vitamin D.

For fat loss
- Protein to preserve muscle; caffeine if desired for energy; avoid unnecessary calorie-containing supplements.

Recovery-focused
- Protein post-workout; omega-3; adequate magnesium and sleep; consider tart cherry for soreness (evidence mixed but low risk).

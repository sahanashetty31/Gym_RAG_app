Meal planning for body composition goals

Lose fat (caloric deficit)
- Target 0.5–1 lb/week loss. Create a 300–500 kcal deficit.
- Prioritize protein: 1.6–2.2 g per kg bodyweight to preserve muscle.
- Fill the rest with vegetables, moderate carbs around training, healthy fats.
- Example day: Breakfast – eggs, spinach, whole-grain toast. Lunch – grilled chicken salad, olive oil. Dinner – white fish, broccoli, sweet potato. Snacks – Greek yogurt, fruit.

Maintain (body recomposition)
- Calories at or near maintenance. Protein 1.6–2.0 g per kg.
- Spread protein across 3–4 meals. Carbs around workout.
- Example: Oatmeal with berries and protein powder; chicken rice and vegetables; salmon with quinoa and greens; cottage cheese and fruit.

Gain muscle (surplus)
- Small surplus: 200–400 kcal. Protein 1.8–2.4 g per kg.
- Carbs post-workout. Fats moderate for hormones.
- Example: Large breakfast with eggs and oats; pre-workout banana and rice; post-workout chicken, rice, vegetables; dinner with lean meat and potatoes; evening casein or cottage cheese.

High-protein meal ideas
- Chicken breast, turkey, lean beef, fish (salmon, cod, tilapia), eggs, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, tofu, lentils, edamame.
- Pair with complex carbs: brown rice, quinoa, sweet potato, oats, whole-grain bread.

Low-carb options for cutting
- Base meals on protein + non-starchy vegetables + healthy fats (avocado, nuts, olive oil).
- Limit grains and sugars; keep carbs around training if needed for performance.
